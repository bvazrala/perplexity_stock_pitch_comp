## 2. How the $650B Breaks Down Into Layers

### The Stack Decomposition

Google provided the clearest public breakdown on its Q4 2025 call: CFO Anat Ashkenazi confirmed Alphabet is **"allocating roughly 60% of capex to servers and 40% to data centers and networking equipment."** Microsoft's Amy Hood said two-thirds of its $37.5B quarterly capex goes to "short-lived assets, primarily GPUs and CPUs," with the remainder going to "long-lived assets" (physical data center infrastructure and networking).

| Layer | % of Total Capex | Implied $ (2026 Combined) |
|---|---|---|
| Compute (GPUs, CPUs, custom silicon) | ~55–60% | ~$360–390B |
| Data center build (power, cooling, real estate) | ~25–30% | ~$163–195B |
| **Networking (switches, cables, optics, retimers)** | **~8–13%** | **~$52–85B** |

The networking band is the direct addressable pool for companies like CRDO.

## 3. Within Networking, How Much Flows to CRDO Specifically?

### The Sub-Layer Breakdown

Not all of the $65B in networking flows to connectivity silicon. It breaks into:

| Sub-Layer | % of Networking Budget | Implied $ | CRDO Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Switches (Tomahawk, Tofino, custom) | ~35–40% | ~$23–26B | None direct |
| Optical transceivers (800G, 1.6T) | ~30–35% | ~$20–23B | ZeroFlap, Robin DSP |
| Active electrical cables (AECs) | ~8–12% | ~$5–8B | Direct — AEC retimers |
| Retimers / DSP silicon ICs | ~10–15% | ~$6–10B | Direct — IC products |
| Network management software | ~5–10% | ~$3–6B | None direct |

CRDO currently captures the **AEC retimer** and **optical DSP/retimer** sub-layers. With ZeroFlap shipping, it is beginning to move up into the full **optical transceiver** category — 3–4x larger dollar content per connection than just the IC inside it.

### CRDO's Estimated Share at Each Layer

| Product Category | Est. CRDO Share | Addressable Pool | Implied Revenue Ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| AEC retimers (4–5 hyperscalers) | ~65% | ~$6–8B | ~$4–5B |
| Optical DSP / retimers | ~20% | ~$6–10B | ~$1.2–2B |
| ZeroFlap transceivers (ramping) | ~0% today → scaling FY2027 | ~$20–23B | Multi-billion long-term |

**CRDO's actual annualized revenue ($1.63B) = ~2.5% of the $65B base-case networking TAM.**  
FY2027 estimate (~$2.4B) = ~3.7% of the same TAM. Still early innings.

### Key Quote from CRDO Q3 FY2026 Earnings Call (March 2, 2026)

> *"Our AEC product line once again delivered strong growth driven by existing customers and new wins, including our fifth hyperscaler. Demand is accelerating across both hyperscalers and emerging neocloud providers. We continue to believe the industry is early in its AEC adoption."*

> *"From fiscal 2024 to fiscal 2025, we more than doubled revenue. From fiscal 2025 to current year fiscal 2026, we expect to triple revenue on top of that. That represents greater than 6x growth in just two years."*

## 4. What the Earnings Calls Say Specifically About Connectivity

### Microsoft — Q2 FY2026 (January 28, 2026)

Satya Nadella described connecting Atlanta and Wisconsin data centers **"through an AI WAN to build a first-of-its-kind AI super factory,"** adding nearly **1 GW of capacity in the quarter alone**. Amy Hood added:

> *"continued replacement of end-of-life server and networking equipment"*

as a key capex driver — signaling that **networking refresh cycles, not just new builds**, are a persistent revenue stream for connectivity vendors. Microsoft's $37.5B quarterly capex is the largest single-quarter infrastructure spend in the history of any company.

Additional context from the call:

> *"The remaining spend was for long-lived assets that will support monetization for the next 15 years and beyond."* — Amy Hood

> *"We have an $80 billion backlog of Azure orders that cannot be fulfilled due to power constraints, suggesting demand is outpacing even its aggressive build-out pace."*

### Google / Alphabet — Q4 2025 (February 4, 2026)

Sundar Pichai explicitly stated Alphabet will be *"among the first to offer the latest Vera Rubin GPU platform"* — the very architecture that requires 1.6T interconnects. He also confirmed:

> *"We have the industry's widest variety of compute options... Our 10-year track record in building our own accelerators with expertise in chips, systems, **networking**, and software translates to leading power and performance efficiency."*

CFO Anat Ashkenazi on the capex allocation:

> *"Total cost of revenue was $45.8 billion, up 13%... with the increase primarily driven by depreciation associated with the deployment of our technical infrastructure."*

Cloud backlog surged **55% sequentially to $240B** — a forward demand signal that networking infrastructure buildout will continue at pace through at least 2027.

### Amazon — Q4 2025 (February 5, 2026)

Andy Jassy on infrastructure investment:

> *"We expect to invest about $200 billion in capital expenditures across Amazon, but predominantly in AWS... Customers really want AWS for core and AI workloads, and we're monetizing capacity as fast as we can install it."*

CFO Brian Olsavsky specifically called out networking efficiency as a cost lever:

> *"This includes investing in software and process improvements to optimize server capacity, **developing a more efficient network using our lower cost custom networking gear**, and advancing custom silicon."*

"More efficient network using lower cost custom networking gear" is a direct reference to AEC-style solutions displacing more expensive optical alternatives at short reach — CRDO's core value proposition.

AWS added **more than 1 GW of capacity in Q4 2025 alone** and grew 24% YoY to a $142B annualized run rate.

### Meta — Q4 2025 (January 28, 2026)

Susan Li on capex composition:

> *"Capital expenditures, including principal payments on finance leases, were $22.1 billion, driven by investments in **data centers, servers, and network infrastructure**."*

> *"The majority of expense growth will be driven by infrastructure costs, which includes third-party cloud spend, **higher depreciation**, and higher infrastructure operating expenses."*

Mark Zuckerberg introduced "Meta Compute":

> *"I recently announced Meta Compute with the belief that being the most efficient at how we engineer, invest, and partner to build our infrastructure will become a strategic advantage."*

> *"An important part of Meta Compute will be making long-term investments in **silicon and energy**. We will continue working with key partners while advancing our own silicon program. We're architecting our systems so we can be flexible in the systems that we use."*

Meta's 1 GW Ohio data center and planned 5 GW Louisiana facility represent multi-year AEC adoption pipelines. Meta is likely one of CRDO's top-3 customers based on revenue concentration disclosures.

### CRDO — Q3 FY2026 (March 2, 2026) — On Hyperscaler Relationships

Bill Brennan on AEC dominance:

> *"Our ZeroFlap AECs deliver up to 1,000x better reliability than commodity laser-based optics, while consuming roughly half the power. In XPU clusters where downtime can cost $millions, network reliability matters."*

> *"AECs are now the de facto standard for intra-rack and rack-to-rack connectivity up to seven meters, increasingly displacing laser-based optical modules."*

Dan Fleming (CFO) on customer concentration:

> *"Our top three end customers were each greater than 10% of revenue in third quarter. We continue to expect that three to four customers will be greater than 10% of revenue in the coming quarters."*

On ZeroFlap qualification:

> *"We're in qualification with three additional customers, including hyperscalers and neocloud operators."*

On CPO competitive risk (in response to analyst question about NVIDIA's optics investments):

> *"Until NPO and CPO solutions can deliver bulletproof reliability, deployments are gonna be somewhat limited, which is why many of the forecasters show low single digit share in the switching market over the next three years. Our investments are heavily focused on reliability."* — Bill Brennan

## 7. The Key Structural Insight

The hyperscaler earnings calls make one thing unmistakably clear: all four are **supply-constrained, not demand-constrained**.

- **Microsoft** has an $80B backlog of Azure orders it cannot fulfill due to power constraints
- **Google's** backlog surged 55% sequentially to $240B
- **Amazon** expects to double its power capacity again by 2027
- **Meta** is building a facility that could eventually scale to **5 GW**

This is not a cyclical build. It is a structural, multi-year infrastructure sprint. Every watt of power added to a rack requires connectivity that keeps pace. The $650B combined hyperscaler capex wave is the tailwind CRDO is riding — and at 2.5% of the networking TAM, the company is not constrained by the size of the market. It is constrained only by its own ability to qualify, ramp, and ship.

> *"AI workloads continue to grow in parameter size, model complexity, and cluster scale, driving sustained transitions from 100 gig to 200 gig per lane and to 400 gig per lane in the upcoming years. We believe the industry's persistent push towards higher speed and larger clusters continues to expand our long-term opportunity."*
> — Bill Brennan, CRDO CEO, Q3 FY2026 Earnings Call
